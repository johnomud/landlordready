The Government Information Sheet explains the new Renters' Rights Act 2025 tenancy rules to tenants. Every landlord with an existing tenancy must provide this to each tenant by 31 May 2026. Update your tenancy agreement template — remove all fixed-term and Section 21 clauses before issuing any new tenancy from 1 May 2026
⏰ Before any new tenancy from 1 May 2026 ⚠ Invalid documents; risk of losing possession rights Tenancy Documents
From 1 May 2026 you cannot enter into a new fixed-term Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST). All new tenancies must be Assured Periodic Tenancies (APTs) — open-ended with no specified end date. Using a fixed-term agreement after this date is unlawful. Section 21 clauses in your agreement are also void from this date. If you use a template downloaded or purchased before May 2026, you must update it before issuing any new tenancy. The £29 document pack includes a fully updated template.
Learn the Section 8 possession grounds before 1 May 2026 — this becomes your only legal route to recover a property
⏰ Before 1 May 2026 ⚠ Without this knowledge you may be unable to recover possession when needed Possession & Eviction
Section 21 'no-fault' evictions are abolished from 1 May 2026. After that date, the only way to recover possession of a property is through the Section 8 process, using one of the statutory grounds set out in Schedule 2 to the Housing Act 1988 (as amended). Key grounds include: Ground 8 (3+ months rent arrears — mandatory), Ground 1A (landlord wishes to sell — 4 months notice, not available in first 12 months of tenancy), and Ground 14 (anti-social behaviour). Evidence is required for all grounds. Start familiarising yourself with these now, before you need them urgently.

Switch to the formal Section 13 process for all rent increases from 1 May 2026 — verbal or informal increases become unlawful
📅 From 1 May 2026 Rent & Deposits
Your answers indicate you currently increase rent informally or verbally. From 1 May 2026 this is no longer lawful. All rent increases must be served using a Section 13 notice (Form 4A), with a minimum of 2 months' written notice, and can only be applied once every 12 months. Tenants have the right to challenge any increase at the First-tier Tribunal (Property Chamber) if they believe it exceeds market rent. Informal or verbal increases after 1 May 2026 are void.
Obtain a valid Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) for any property where this is not confirmed — and provide a copy to each tenant
📅 Before next new tenancy Safety & Standards
You did not confirm a valid Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) in your compliance checklist. All privately rented properties in England have been required to have a valid Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) since April 2021. It must be carried out by a qualified electrician every 5 years (or sooner if recommended), and a copy must be provided to each tenant within 28 days of the inspection. A copy must also be given to any new tenant before they move in. Local councils can issue civil penalties of up to £30,000 for non-compliance.
